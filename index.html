Unit 1: Getting Started

Introduction & Mindset, DAACS

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 1, I was introduced to the concept of self-regulated learning through the DAACS assessment. As a Navy veteran transitioning to civilian life and a new career as a data analyst, this assessment provided valuable insights into my learning strengths and areas for improvement. I discovered that while I excel at creating a distraction-free study environment, I face challenges with time management, help-seeking, and test anxiety.

My DAACS report highlighted how my military background influenced my approach to learning. After 23 years in the Navy as an Aviation Machinists Mate, I developed a habit of operating under pressure and prioritizing others' needs over my own. This assessment helped me understand that what worked in a high-tempo military environment might not be the most effective approach for academic success.

The mindset discussion revealed that I possess a strong growth mindset in many areas, particularly in my professional capabilities, but also showed how my background as a non-native English speaker sometimes affects my confidence in academic settings.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to leverage the DAACS assessment results as a roadmap for improving my academic strategies. Now that I understand my strengths and weaknesses, I can take specific actions to address areas needing development:

  • For help-seeking, I'll practice overcoming my reluctance to ask questions by reminding myself that seeking assistance isn't a burden but can actually benefit both myself and others. I'll use the resources UMGC provides, such as tutoring services and the Effective Writing Center.
  • For time management, I'll implement structured planning using the techniques suggested in the DAACS resources, including creating consistent study schedules and prioritizing assignments based on importance and deadline.
  • For test anxiety, I'll utilize relaxation techniques before and during high-stress situations and focus on consistent preparation rather than last-minute cramming.
Key Takeaways
  • Self-awareness is the foundation of personal growth. The DAACS assessment provided objective feedback that helped me see patterns in my learning behaviors that I might not have recognized on my own.
  • My background in the Navy gave me valuable skills like problem-solving and persistence, but also created habits that may need adjustment in an academic environment.
  • As I mentioned in my introduction post, my approach to troubleshooting aircraft engines—breaking down complex problems into manageable parts—can be applied to my academic journey. Just as I would "take a step back, assess the situation, and figure out how everything was supposed to come together" when faced with mechanical issues, I can apply this same methodical approach to overcoming learning challenges.
  • The path from military service to data science represents not just a career change but an opportunity to combine my technical expertise with new academic knowledge in ways that align with my natural curiosity and problem-solving abilities.
  • I recognize that this self-assessment is not just about identifying weaknesses but about building on my existing strengths to create a solid foundation for success at UMGC and in my data science career.

Unit 2: Setting Direction

Goals & Motivations

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 2, I delved into the process of setting effective goals and understanding my core motivations. Through creating SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound), I learned how to transform vague aspirations into concrete, actionable plans. This exercise helped me identify my core values—Purpose, Happiness, Wisdom, Achievement, and Creativity—which serve as the foundation for my personal and professional decisions.

I discovered that my motivation stems from multiple sources. For my academic goals, my family—particularly my wife and children—provides significant inspiration, as I want to create a better future for them. For my professional development goals, my transition from aviation mechanics to data analysis represents an opportunity to apply my existing work ethic to a new field. And for my personal growth goals, I recognized the need to invest in myself after decades of prioritizing others during my Naval service.

Creating action items for each goal taught me the importance of breaking down larger objectives into manageable steps. For instance, establishing a weekly study schedule, researching certification options, and setting up meditation reminders are all concrete actions that support my broader ambitions.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement the SMART goal framework for all significant objectives in my academic, professional, and personal life:

  • I'll continue to follow the academic SMART goal of completing 18 credits toward my Data Science degree over the next 12 months, using the specific weekly planning system I established.
  • I'll pursue the professional development goal of completing the Google Analytics certification, dedicating three hours weekly to this endeavor to enhance my data analysis capabilities.
  • I'll maintain my personal growth goal of daily meditation and mindfulness reading to ensure I'm cultivating self-care practices that support my overall well-being.

I'll revisit these goals regularly, perhaps monthly, to assess my progress and make adjustments as needed. The action items I created will serve as my roadmap, helping me stay on track even when faced with competing priorities.

Key Takeaways
  • The SMART framework transforms abstract aspirations into tangible plans with clear metrics for success. This structure is particularly valuable for someone like me who is transitioning from military service—where goals were often externally imposed—to civilian life where I must set and pursue my own objectives.
  • My goals across different life domains are interconnected. Academic success supports career advancement, which provides for my family, while personal well-being enables sustained performance in both academic and professional areas.
  • The action items are just as important as the goals themselves. Without specific implementation plans, even the best-formulated goals remain unrealized. The concrete steps I outlined for each goal create accountability and a clear path forward.
  • My values of achievement and purpose are deeply connected to my family's well-being and my professional growth, while wisdom and happiness relate more to my personal development needs after years of service.
  • The transition from military to civilian life represents not just a career change but an opportunity to recalibrate my approach to goal-setting and personal development, balancing external achievements with internal growth.

This unit helped me understand that effective goal-setting isn't just about determining what I want to accomplish, but about aligning those objectives with my core values and creating systematic plans for achievement.

Unit 3: Time Management

Big Rocks & Scheduling

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 3, I gained critical insights into how I allocate my time and learned practical strategies for prioritizing what truly matters. Through the time tracking exercise, I discovered discrepancies between how I thought I spent my time versus my actual time usage. The most revealing insights were that I spent an hour more sleeping than estimated, skipped my planned exercise hour entirely, spent one hour less with family, and devoted an hour more to schoolwork than anticipated.

This unit introduced me to the concept of "big rocks" - the most important priorities that should be placed first in our schedules before less significant tasks fill the gaps. This metaphor resonated deeply with my military experience. As I shared in my discussion post about managing multiple aircraft engine repairs during deployment, I've faced situations where identifying and focusing on "big rocks" was mission-critical. The parallel between prioritizing engine repairs on a ship and managing academic, professional, and personal commitments became clear.

I also learned practical scheduling techniques by creating a weekly calendar that visualized my time commitments. This visual representation helped me see where my time was going and identify opportunities to better align my time allocation with my stated priorities of family, schoolwork, and exercise.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement several strategies based on what I learned in Unit 3:

  • I'll adjust my sleep schedule to wake up at 5 AM instead of 6 AM, freeing up an hour for exercise that I previously neglected despite identifying it as a top-three priority.
  • I'll expand my use of planning tools beyond just work-related Outlook scheduling to include all areas of my life, ensuring that my "big rocks" of family time, schoolwork, and exercise are scheduled first.
  • I'll apply the prioritization techniques I used during high-pressure military situations to my academic and personal life. Just as I strategically allocated resources when faced with multiple engine repairs, I'll focus my energy on high-impact academic tasks first, while finding ways to manage routine responsibilities efficiently.
  • I'll conduct regular time audits (similar to the exercise in this unit) to ensure my actual time usage aligns with my priorities, making adjustments as needed to maintain alignment between my stated values and how I spend my hours.
Key Takeaways
  • Time management isn't just about efficiency—it's about intentionality. As I wrote in my reflection, time management means "balancing priorities to ensure that I allocate my resources (primarily time and energy) in a way that aligns with both immediate responsibilities and long-term goals."
  • The discrepancies between estimated and actual time spent on activities reveal unconscious patterns that may work against my stated priorities. Awareness of these patterns is the first step toward meaningful change.
  • My military experience provided valuable lessons in prioritization that I can apply to civilian life. The strategies I used during that challenging week with multiple engine repairs—focused resource allocation, seeking external support, rescheduling less critical tasks, and efficient task management—are directly applicable to balancing academic, professional, and personal commitments.
  • Creating a visual schedule helps identify time blocks that aren't being used optimally and ensures that priority activities receive dedicated time slots rather than being pushed aside by urgent but less important matters.

This unit reinforced that effective time management isn't about cramming more activities into each day, but about ensuring the most important responsibilities—the "big rocks"—receive the time and attention they deserve. By applying the strategic prioritization skills I developed in the military to my current civilian and academic life, I can better align my daily actions with my long-term goals.

Unit 4: Building Support

Success Network

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 4, I learned about the importance of building and utilizing a support network at UMGC. The assignment required me to engage with my success network partner, which highlighted the value of proactive communication and preparation when seeking academic guidance. This experience taught me that having a plan before meetings with advisors is crucial—identifying specific questions in advance helps make the interaction more productive.

I discovered the various resources available through the UMGC success network, including success coaches, military education coordinators (MECs), advisors, and program coordinators. Through my interaction with Carvarie Bennett, I learned how these professionals can provide specific guidance on academic planning, course selection, program requirements, and administrative processes like tuition payment through the Post 9/11 GI Bill.

The assignment also introduced me to the structured approach of planning, meeting, reflecting, and taking action—a methodology that can be applied not just to academic advising but to many professional interactions. Despite experiencing some initial communication challenges with scheduling my first appointment, I learned to be persistent and find alternative solutions, such as calling the 1-800 number to get my questions answered.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement several strategies based on what I learned in Unit 4:

  • I'll maintain regular contact with my success network partners, scheduling check-ins at key points during each semester rather than waiting until I have urgent questions. This proactive approach will help me stay on track with my academic plan.
  • I'll continue preparing detailed questions before each meeting, organizing them by priority to ensure the most critical items are addressed first. As demonstrated in my assignment, having specific questions about my minor, course requirements, and summer classes led to valuable guidance.
  • I'll follow a similar plan-meet-reflect-action framework for other important academic and professional interactions, taking the time to document outcomes and next steps to ensure follow-through.
  • I'll be more assertive in following up when communication breaks down, as I did when my initial appointment wasn't confirmed. Rather than waiting and potentially missing important information, I'll take initiative to resolve issues promptly.
  • I'll explore additional support services mentioned during my conversation with Carvarie, such as tutoring options, which I noted as a question I wish I had asked.
Key Takeaways
  • The success network at UMGC is a valuable resource that can provide personalized guidance tailored to my specific academic needs and military background. As a veteran using the Post 9/11 GI Bill, having knowledgeable advisors who understand these specific processes is invaluable.
  • Preparation is essential for productive advising sessions. My list of specific questions about declaring a minor, understanding course requirements, and planning for summer classes enabled a focused conversation despite initial scheduling challenges.
  • Persistence pays off when navigating administrative processes. When my initial meeting didn't materialize as planned, I found alternative ways to get my questions answered rather than simply waiting for a response.
  • The detailed follow-up actions I outlined in my assignment—such as reviewing emails about minors, evaluating options based on career goals, and systematically selecting courses—demonstrate the importance of converting advice into concrete steps.
  • Even well-planned interactions can have gaps. By reflecting on what questions I wished I had asked (about tutoring and strategies for staying focused during virtual classes), I learned to continuously improve my approach to seeking support.

This unit reinforced that academic success isn't solely an individual endeavor but is enhanced through strategic engagement with the university's support systems. By understanding how to effectively tap into these resources, I can navigate my educational journey more confidently and efficiently.

Unit 5: Career Exploration

Industry Trends & Interview Prep

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 5, I gained valuable insights into the current landscape of the data science field and learned effective strategies for preparing for professional networking conversations. The assignment required me to use both AI tools and traditional internet research to identify trends in my industry, which revealed the growing importance of AI and machine learning, automation and augmented analytics, and data governance and ethics within the field of data science.

This research process taught me how to critically evaluate information from different sources. By comparing AI-generated content with information from reputable websites like Coursera, GeeksforGeeks, and Litslink, I learned to identify both similarities and differences in how trends are presented. This comparative analysis helped me develop a more nuanced understanding of my field and recognize potential biases or gaps in any single information source.

Through the discussion post, I explored the value of informational interviews for career development. I learned to strategically select interview candidates who can provide insights aligned with my career transition goals—in this case, moving from my current role as a Data Analyst to becoming a Data Scientist. By identifying Chris Davis, my current supervisor and VP at VTG Defense's V-22 Modernization Group, I recognized the importance of leveraging existing connections who can provide both industry knowledge and personalized guidance.

The preparation process taught me to anticipate challenges and develop strategies to navigate complex conversations effectively. I learned that even when interviewing someone who isn't in my target role (Chris is not a Data Scientist), their strategic insights and industry experience can still provide valuable perspective for my career development.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement several strategies based on what I learned in Unit 5:

  • I'll regularly research industry trends using multiple sources, including both AI tools and credible industry publications, to stay current in the rapidly evolving field of data science. This will help me identify emerging skills I need to develop, such as the advanced programming and machine learning techniques highlighted in my research.
  • I'll cultivate my professional network by conducting informational interviews with individuals who can provide different perspectives on data science—both technical experts and strategic leaders. The questions I developed about transitioning from data analyst to data scientist will serve as starting points for these conversations.
  • I'll create a skills development plan that aligns with the industry trends I identified, focusing on AI and machine learning, automation and analytics, and data governance and ethics. This will help me prepare for my desired career transition.
  • I'll develop a more comprehensive preparation strategy for professional conversations, researching the background and projects of individuals before meetings to ask informed questions that demonstrate genuine interest and create meaningful dialogue.
  • I'll practice balancing technical questions with strategic discussions when speaking with leaders who may not have technical expertise in data science but can provide valuable insights on its business applications.
Key Takeaways
  • The field of data science is rapidly evolving, with AI, machine learning, and data ethics emerging as critical areas of focus. To remain competitive, I need to continuously update my skills and knowledge in these domains.
  • Information from different sources can vary in focus and depth, making it essential to consult multiple resources when researching industry trends. The differences I found between AI-generated content and internet research highlighted the importance of a well-rounded approach to gathering information.
  • Strategic networking is crucial for career transitions. As I noted in my discussion post, even though Chris Davis isn't a Data Scientist, his leadership experience and strategic insights make him a valuable resource for understanding how data science principles are applied at an organizational level.
  • Preparation is key to successful informational interviews. As I identified in my reflection, researching the interviewee's background and projects beforehand demonstrates genuine interest and facilitates meaningful dialogue.
  • Career development requires both technical skill-building and strategic networking. The questions I developed for my informational interview—focusing on additional skills to develop and experiences that would enhance my portfolio—reflect this dual approach.

This unit has equipped me with practical tools for researching my field and preparing for professional conversations that will support my career transition from Data Analyst to Data Scientist, building on my unique background as a Navy veteran who transitioned from aircraft mechanics to data analysis.

Unit 6: Professional Connections

Networking & Informational Interview

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 6, I gained valuable insights into the power of professional networking and the importance of effective teamwork. Through my informational interview with David Light, Technical Director for the Advanced Programs Group at VTG Defense, I experienced firsthand how networking can create unexpected opportunities. What began as a planned interview with my supervisor, Chris Davis, transformed into a connection with a machine learning expert whose expertise aligned perfectly with my career aspirations in data science.

This experience taught me the importance of flexibility and authenticity in professional conversations. When my carefully prepared questions suddenly seemed irrelevant for my new interviewee, I learned to adapt quickly by sharing my own journey—from my 23 years as a Navy aircraft mechanic to my nine-month experience as a data analyst. This personal sharing created a foundation for a genuine connection that transformed a scheduled 20-minute interview into an engaging and informative conversation about tools, processes, and career development.

The unit's focus on teamwork resonated strongly with my military experience. In my discussion post about the multinational military exercise in Hawaii, I reflected on how our diverse team of aircraft maintainers—each with different technical specialties—collaborated to replace a reduction gearbox while simultaneously handling an unexpected fuel leak. This challenging situation highlighted how effective teamwork requires breaking complex tasks into manageable steps, building confidence through progressive skill development, and maintaining clear communication throughout the process.

I learned that similar principles apply to both military teamwork and professional networking: building trust, adapting to unexpected situations, sharing knowledge across traditional boundaries, and focusing on a common goal. Just as I guided my maintenance team through complex repairs by starting with small confidence-building tasks, I found that my networking conversation with David was most productive when we established common ground before delving into specialized topics.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement several strategies based on what I learned in Unit 6:

  • I'll approach networking with greater flexibility and openness, recognizing that the most valuable connections may emerge unexpectedly. My experience with David showed how a conversation can evolve beyond its initial purpose into something more valuable when both parties are genuinely engaged.
  • I'll utilize the resources David recommended—particularly Medium for staying current with industry developments and roadmap.sh for structured learning paths—to continue developing my technical skills in data science and machine learning.
  • I'll apply the teamwork principles from my military experience to professional settings, particularly the approach of building confidence through progressive skill development and maintaining clear communication during complex projects.
  • I'll be more intentional about seeking mentorship relationships, as my conversation with David demonstrated how much can be learned from someone with expertise in my desired career path. His insights about balancing technical knowledge with strong communication skills will guide my professional development.
  • I'll leverage my unique background transitioning from aircraft maintenance to data analysis as a strength when networking, as this unconventional path provides me with diverse perspectives that can be valuable in collaborative environments.
Key Takeaways
  • The most valuable professional connections often develop organically. While preparation is important, being adaptable and authentic in the moment can transform a structured interview into a meaningful mentoring relationship, as happened with David Light.
  • Technical expertise alone is insufficient for career success. Both my informational interview and team leadership experience highlighted that communication skills, strategic thinking, and the ability to explain complex concepts in understandable terms are equally important.
  • Cross-domain knowledge can be a significant advantage. As I noted in my discussion post, teaching aircraft maintainers from different specialties to work on engine components built their confidence and expanded their capabilities. Similarly, my conversation with David revealed how my mechanical background provides a unique perspective that can be valuable in data science.
  • Continuous learning is essential in rapidly evolving fields. David's emphasis on staying current through resources like Medium reinforced the importance of ongoing education beyond formal degree programs.
  • Successful teamwork and networking share common foundations: trust, clear communication, shared goals, and mutual respect. Whether coordinating a complex aircraft repair or building a professional relationship, these elements create an environment where collaboration can flourish.

This unit reinforced that professional success is not achieved in isolation but through meaningful connections with others and effective collaboration. My experiences in both military teamwork and professional networking have provided me with a strong foundation for building relationships that will support my career transition and ongoing growth in the field of data science.

Unit 7: University Resources

Support Services

What Did I Learn?

In Unit 7, I deepened my understanding of the comprehensive support system available at UMGC and learned how to strategically utilize these resources to enhance my academic journey. Through researching and writing about the Success Network, Wellness Resources, and Tutoring Services, I gained valuable insights into how these services can address different dimensions of student needs—from academic guidance to mental well-being and specialized subject matter support.

I discovered that the Success Network functions as a central hub connecting students with various academic support services, providing a structured way to communicate with instructors and advisors when facing challenges. My previous interactions with this service had already demonstrated its practical value in maintaining academic momentum, but the assignment helped me articulate more clearly how it serves as a foundation for student success.

The unit also highlighted the importance of wellness resources, particularly for students like myself who are transitioning from military to civilian life. I learned that UMGC provides access to mental health professionals who understand the unique challenges faced by veterans, offering support for managing stress during exam periods, adjusting to new civilian routines, and balancing academic demands with major life transitions.

Additionally, I gained a more comprehensive understanding of UMGC's Tutoring Services and how they can be strategically utilized for challenging subjects in my data science program. The research for this assignment helped me anticipate future academic challenges in advanced statistics and programming concepts, and plan how to leverage subject matter experts through one-on-one tutoring sessions.

How Will I Use This Moving Forward?

Moving forward, I plan to implement several strategies based on what I learned in Unit 7:

  • I'll proactively engage with the Success Network at strategic points throughout each semester—not just when problems arise. This might include scheduling regular check-ins with my advisor to ensure I'm on track with my academic plan and career goals in data science.
  • I'll incorporate wellness practices into my regular routine, utilizing UMGC's Wellness Resources during potentially stressful periods like midterms and finals. Recognizing that my transition from military to civilian life presents unique challenges, I'll view mental health support as an essential component of academic success.
  • I'll create a tutoring plan for challenging courses in my data science program, identifying in advance which subjects might require additional support. Rather than waiting until I'm struggling with complex programming or statistical concepts, I'll schedule tutoring sessions early in the semester to build a strong foundation.
  • I'll maintain a comprehensive list of all available support services with contact information and access procedures, ensuring I can quickly connect with the right resource when needs arise.
  • I'll share information about these resources with fellow student veterans, recognizing that many might not be aware of the specialized support available for those transitioning from military service to academic life.
Key Takeaways
  • Support services at UMGC are designed to address the whole student experience—academic, personal, and professional. By understanding how these services complement each other, I can create a comprehensive support system tailored to my specific needs as a veteran pursuing a data science degree.
  • Proactive engagement with support services is more effective than reactive usage. Planning ahead for challenging courses, anticipated stress periods, or major transitions allows me to leverage these resources strategically rather than only in crisis situations.
  • The transition from military to civilian life presents unique challenges that extend beyond academics. UMGC's Wellness Resources acknowledge this reality and provide specialized support that recognizes the distinct experiences of veteran students.
  • As I progress into more advanced technical courses in my data science program, the availability of specialized tutoring will become increasingly important. Having subject matter experts to consult about complex programming and statistical concepts will be crucial for maintaining academic excellence.
  • Support services not only help address immediate challenges but also build long-term skills for success. Whether through improving study techniques, developing stress management strategies, or mastering technical concepts, these resources contribute to both present academic performance and future professional capabilities.

This unit reinforced that seeking support is not a sign of weakness but a strategic approach to academic success. By understanding and utilizing the comprehensive resources available at UMGC, I can navigate challenges more effectively and maximize my potential as I pursue my data science degree and transition to civilian professional life.
